What is a Maternity Care Desert?

March of Dimes defines a maternity care desert as “counties in which access to maternity health care services is limited or absent, either through lack of services or barriers to a woman’s ability to access that care”. When defining deserts, it’s important to consider access to maternity care, hospitals with obstetric care, OB-GYN provider rates, and reproductive health insurance coverage.

The map below examines deserts as the number of counties that lack National Health Service Corps designated centres that offer maternity care.

While the visualization does offer a baseline perspective on the lack of direct access parents across the US have to maternity health care services, it’s important to keep in mind the population of counties/states and the capacity of these healthcare centres, size of the counties, quality and inclusiveness of care provided, and many other factors that prevent parents from accessing these necessary resources.

For example, the US has an evident scarcity of reproductive care centres that are informed, sensitive, and tailored to the needs of patients who are:

  • people of colour
  • LGBTQ+
  • gender non-conforming/expansive
  • immigrants
  • low-income
  • living in a rural area
